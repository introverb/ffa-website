Become a Corporate Patron.

For companies building toward a future worth wanting.

FFA is a 501(c)(3) building cultural infrastructure for optimistic, realistic futures. We fund the artists, scientists, and writers doing that work, and we make those futures legible, desirable, and lasting.

A corporate patron backs the foundation directly, through a strategic, tax-deductible charitable donation. This is not event sponsorship. You are not buying a placement on a single evening. You become a patron of the institution at the moment it launches, and your support shows up first in public at OURS.

Why companies become patrons
  • Category alignment. Your brand stands beside a cultural institution making the case for the future, rather than against it.
  • Founding position. You are named among the first patrons of FFA, permanently.
  • Real placement, real media. Named credit across the foundation’s materials, plus professional photography and video of OURS that you can use in your own channels.
The Founding Circle

We are closing our founding circle of corporate patrons on August 9, the night of OURS.

Companies that join before then are named as founding patrons in the foundation’s permanent record and announced at the debut. Treat it the way you would an early round: the position is strongest at the start, and it is only available once.

What a Corporate Patron receives
  • Named credit as a Founding Corporate Patron across the OURS event page, printed program, and event signage.
  • Professional photography and video of OURS, deliverable for your marketing use.
  • An invitation for two or more guests to the August 9 evening, a curated room of 70 to 80 artists, scientists, writers, and builders.
  • Permanent placement on the FFA patrons page.
  • A commissioned Possibilia story featuring your industry, with the opportunity to author the nonfiction companion piece. A full editorial package, equivalent to a Possibilia sponsorship, included.
  • Recognition in the inaugural credits of Possibilia.
  • Real, tax-deductible charitable support of a 501(c)(3).
